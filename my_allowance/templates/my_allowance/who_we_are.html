What We do

The learning environment and its impact on learning

Children quickly pick up on ethos – norms and expectations (which is not to say they always conform to these). Children growing up need to learn how to work hard and how to make good smart money decisions. Ensuring a positive, shared ethos is a high priority because of its importance for the life of the community and its impact on learning. In learning environments where the ethos supports learning:


There is an expectation on the part of both adults and children that learning is important and enjoyable, and that everyone can achieve;
Teaching uses a range of approaches and there is a culture of collaborative learning;
Children are motivated to be ‘the best that they can be’.

Technology offers a learning medium with distinctive features. These can be used to support and enhance the development of learners. While ICT can be used to support teaching in many and varied contexts, successful use reflects the extent to which teachers or practitioners interact with children, Successful use of ICT can:


Introduce greater variety and stimuli that capture children’s interest, generate enthusiasm and prompt ideas and thinking;
Provide improved access to different media and resources that help children to communicate and explore;
Accelerate learning

 

Using technology, we disrupt traditional development opportunities and customize learning with increased participation based on collaboration. Students will expect more opportunities, get their work ethic on and prepare them for the realities ahead.

As a startup, we concentrate on building home school connections for learning to include parents as partners. In this era, the voice and choice of students should act as guiding practice and pedagogy.
